I am pursuing a B.S. in Geological Sciences at the University of Southern California (USC), 
where I have been conducting research since 2023 in Prof. Sam Silva's 
<a href="https://www.earthdatausc.com/">Atmospheric Composition & Earth Data Science</a> lab. 
My research journey began there, where I developed skills in atmospheric data analysis and 
grew increasingly interested in atmospheric sciences.


In 2025, I participated in the NASA Student Airborne Research Program (SARP), studying methane 
emissions in wetland ecosystems. This experience gave me the opportunity to fly aboard a research 
aircraft and collect airborne measurements firsthand.

I am currently a SOARS (Significant Opportunities in Atmospheric Research and Science) intern 
at NSF NCAR (Summer 2026), where my work focuses on characterizing and correcting biases in 
low-cost PM2.5 air quality sensors deployed across climatically distinct sites. This project 
sits at the intersection of sensor science and environmental justice where I intend to improve the reliability 
of low-cost air quality monitoring in across all communities.
